Effect of gases on temperature

Increased surface temperature: Emissions of greenhouse gases contribute to increased accumulation of these gases in the atmosphere. This leads to greater trapping of solar heat in the atmosphere and an increase in the Earth's surface temperatures

Effects of climate change

An increase in global temperature causes changes in climate that include an increase in the occurrence of extreme weather events such as storms, floods, and sea level rise

Result

.Temperatures in the Suez Industrial Zone were analyzed over the past 30 years in July of each year (2003,2013, 2023)
  • It was found that the industrial areas in Suez are witnessing a continuous rise in temperatures, exceeding 37degrees Celsius during the month of July.
  • On the other hand, temperatures in non-industrial areas remain below 35 degrees Celsius during the same period.
  • These results indicate the impact of industrial activity on increasing temperatures in the region

    Damage of Gases to The Environment

    • Impact on the marine environment: Global warming leads to a rise in water temperature in the seas and oceans, which affects marine organisms and increases carbon dioxide emissions from the seas and oceans
    • Ecosystem change: Increased temperature affects ecosystems, such as changing the flowering and growth dates of plants and animals. This can cause the extinction of many organisms
    • Impact on water resources: Rising temperatures can increase water evaporation and reduce the availability of fresh water for human use and agriculture
    • Increased sea level: Melting glaciers and rising water temperatures lead to an increase in sea level, threatening coastal areas, islands, homes and infrastructure
    • Impact on biological species: Changes in temperature affect biological species and ecological diversity, which can cause the extinction of animal and plant species

    SOLUTIONS

    • Investing in Renewable Energy: Encouraging the use of renewable energy sources such as solar and wind energy can reduce dependence on fossil fuels and decrease carbon dioxide emissions
    • Controlling Factory Emissions: Enforcing stricter laws and regulations on factories to limit their emissions and monitoring compliance
    • Environmental Awareness: Promoting awareness of the importance of the environment and contributing to reducing air and water pollution and ensuring safe waste disposal
    • Promoting Eco-Friendly Transportation: Supporting the use of public transportation, electric cars, and improving infrastructure for bicycles and pedestrians
    • Research and Development: Investing in research and development to develop clean and energy-efficient technologies
    • International Cooperation: Enhancing international cooperation to address climate change issues and adhering to international agreements such as the Paris Agreement
    • Education and Awareness: Encouraging education and awareness about environmental and climate issues to contribute to changing personal and collective behaviors
